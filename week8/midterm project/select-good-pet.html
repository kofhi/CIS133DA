Looking to get a furry friend?
Here's a Guide to Select a Good Pet

  1. Do you (or someone you live with) have allergies?

Safety comes first! You wouldn't want to trigger allergic reactions all the time. You can choose to have "low-allergen" pets to avoid such problems.

  1. Young and energetic or older but calm?

Puppies may be cute but taking care of them take a lot of hard work and training. They will be full of energy and ready ensue chaos. If you're not prepared for that, consider adopting an older dog.

  1. Are you able to handle their personality and temperment?

Different breeds have different personalities, and some dogs have their own unique quirks. Try to interact with them before you adopt to see if they are the right fit for you.

  1. Does your lifestyle allow for you to take care of them?

Some dogs reqiure more attention, exercise, and maintenance than others. If you are usually not at home and cannot handle the extra work, it's best to stay away from those breeds.

  1. Can you financially afford taking care of them?

Taking care of pets will cost you a lot of money to keep them healthy and provide the necessities they need. Some breeds might also need more things than others, so keep that in mind.
