Sonoran Desert Club at Toad Hall

One of CNUW’s six exhibits, Toad Hall is home to a collection of different native wildlife species including Gila Monsters, Desert Tortoises, Toads, Snakes, and many more.

With partnerships with CNUW, Sonoran Desert Club members have the opportunity to share their passion with wildlife, especially animals, to fellow students and community members of all ages by creating interactive table stations during Open House days.

Table Station Types

Animal Showcase Interactive Mini Game Species Discussion
Description Be able to bring out one of Toad Hall’s non-toxic animal residents to help people get to know them more and up close. Have visitors interact and play a type of mini game where they are able to learn interesting facts about the wildlife. Talk about and introduce a biodiversity topic you are passionate about that is not present in Toad Hall.
Table Activities
  • Let people touch and interact with the animal.

  • Tell interesting facts about the species.

  • Tell interesting stories of what the animal does on normal days at CNUW.
  • Host guessing game activities to foster analytical thinking for a deeper understanding of wildlife.

  • Let people play games that focus on the mundane aspects of life to allow visitors to relate and grasp the topic and purpose of species more.
  • Present visual graphics, samples, or any relevant tools to help people visualize and understand the species you are advocating about.

  • Make interesting discussions that explain why your topic is interesting and important.
